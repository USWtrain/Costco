Costco Program Overview
Identity, services, event flow, and logistics
⚠
Identity — critical Introduce yourself and refer to your team as Onsite Wellness screeners at all times. Never identify as US Wellness employees. All participant-facing forms and signage are Onsite Wellness branded.

US Wellness has partnered with Onsite Wellness to deliver Costco's onsite employee health screening program across more than 450 warehouse locations nationwide. All data must be recorded in both the Salesforce tablet and the paper results sheet.

Event flow

  1. 1
    Greet participant
    All participants are walk-ins — no pre-populated list exists in Salesforce.
  2. 2
    Create appointment in Salesforce
    Locate and open the new appointment.
  3. 3
    Digital consent
    Ask participant to complete consent on the tablet.
  4. 4
    Screen participant & enter results
    Record all values in Salesforce and on the paper results sheet.
  5. 5
    Offer Omada Health info
    If interested, record in Salesforce with preferred email address.
  6. 6
    Provide yellow carbon copy
    Participant leaves with the yellow copy. You retain the white copy for USW return.

Dress code & logistics

  • Dress: All black — black scrubs or black pants (no leggings), black button-up, sweater, or collared shirt.
  • Parking: Go to the back door/loading dock. Identify as an "Onsite Wellness" screener. Ask for the site contact. Then park in the main customer lot.
  • Screening room: Events are set up in the break room. Your site contact will direct you.
  • Privacy screens: Not provided for Costco events. Ensure adequate spacing between stations.
  • Teardown: Do not break down until scheduled end time. Bags and boxes (including personal) will be checked at the customer exit.
⚠
Early breakdown You are contractually obligated to remain set up for the entire scheduled shift. If a site contact asks you to leave early, call 866.881.7607 for permission before breaking down.

Module 02
A1c Testing — Bayer A1cNow
Equipment, storage, and 12-step standard operating procedure

What is the A1c test?

The HbA1c result reflects the average blood glucose level over a 2–3 month period, measuring how much glucose has attached to hemoglobin on red blood cells. Because red blood cells have a 120-day lifespan, this provides a reliable long-term average.

  • Monitoring: tracks glucose control in participants already diagnosed with diabetes.
  • Screening: identifies participants at higher risk for developing diabetes.
▶
Required training video Watch the official A1CNow+ training video at ptsdiagnostics.com/a1cnow-training/ before your first event. Key chapters: Test Storage (1:49) · Collecting Blood (3:20) · Running the Test (5:27) · Reading Results (6:57).

Kit contents & storage

Each A1c test kit includes: an analyzer, shaker body, blood collector, and test cartridge (in a separate pouch).

  • Store refrigerated until expiration, OR at room temperature for up to 4 months.
  • Record the date placed at room temperature on the box.
  • If refrigerated, allow product to reach room temperature for at least 1 hour before use.
  • Keep out of direct sunlight. Store all components together; use only components from the original box.

Standard operating procedure

Complete all 12 steps in order. Do not skip or reorder.

  1. 1
    Review kit supplies
    Confirm you have the analyzer, shaker body, blood collector, and test cartridge.
  2. 2
    Confirm room temperature & flat surface
    All equipment must be at room temperature before testing.
  3. 3
    Verify lot numbers match
    The test will error if the analyzer and Pouch 1 lot numbers do not match.
  4. 4
    Open Pouch 1 (sample dilution pouch)
    DO NOT open Pouch 2 (cartridge) yet.
  5. 5
    Perform fingerstick (USW standards)
    The same puncture site may be used if A1c and cholesterol tests run concurrently.
  6. 6
    Collect blood sample
    Touch the blood drop to fill the blood collector — not too little, not too much.
  7. 7
    Insert blood collector into shaker body & shake 6–8 times
    You should hear an audible click when fully inserted.
  8. 8
    Open Pouch 2 & confirm cartridge/analyzer codes match
  9. 9
    Insert cartridge → wait for SMPL → dispense sample
    Remove base of shaker body, push down firmly but gently into the analyzer well. Remove the shaker body quickly once sample is dispensed.
  10. 10
    Do not move analyzer — wait ~5 minutes
    A countdown timer will appear. When complete: QCOK → result → remaining test count.
  11. 11
    Record result in Salesforce and on paper results sheet
  12. 12
    Dispose cartridge in Sharps; save analyzer
    Up to 10 tests per analyzer. After 10, discard analyzer in regular trash.
🚨
QCOK not displayed? Do not report the result. Call the on-call team immediately at 866.881.7607.

Backup & system failure

If the tablet system fails, move to emergency paper consent forms. You must call 866.881.7607 and notify USW before switching to paper consents for Costco events. The Shift Lead must authorize this switch.

📄
Elevated A1c pamphlet For any participant with an A1c of 6.5% or above, provide the informational pamphlet included in your shipment. Do not withhold this resource.

Counseling principles

  • Adjust to the needs of each participant — not everyone requires the same depth of explanation.
  • Provide an accurate overview of what the result means.
  • Be open to questions and feedback.
  • Do NOT say results out loud where others can hear — maintain confidentiality at all times.
  • Make it a great experience — participants should feel supported, not alarmed.

Healthy blood glucose — counseling points

  • Speak with your doctor and follow their therapy plan, especially if you have a diabetes diagnosis.
  • Make healthy food choices and focus on portion control — the amount you eat directly affects blood glucose.
  • Limit carbohydrates and sugary foods. "Sugar free" does not always mean "carbohydrate free."
  • Most simple carbohydrates come from processed foods — soda, cookies, ice cream, cakes.
  • Try to exercise 30–60 minutes per day, most days of the week (check with a doctor before starting a new program).

Module 04
Paperwork & Data Return
Results form handling, data cover sheet, and UPS return procedure

Results form guidelines

  • All results forms are two-ply carbon copies: participant receives the yellow copy; the white top sheet is returned to USW.
  • The participant's name must be clearly written in the designated area at the bottom of every form.
  • The screener's (provider's) name must be clearly written in the designated area at the bottom of every form.
ℹ
Data cover sheet Completed by the Shift Lead after each shift. Must be returned with all paperwork from every event. This sheet helps identify any participant records missing from tablet data entry.

Paperwork return — step by step

  1. 1
    Collect all white results sheets
    Place completed white copies into the clear reusable pocket folder.
  2. 2
    Add the Data Cover Sheet
  3. 3
    Add emergency consent forms (if used)
  4. 4
    Secure folder & place in return supply box
    Secure the string on the folder, then place inside the return supply shipment box.
  5. 5
    Drop off at a manned UPS location
    Drop off within the timeframe provided by your manager. Obtain a receipt with tracking number.
  6. 6
    Submit UPS receipt to your Field Operations Manager
    Include the tracking number in your submission.
📋
Multi-event leads If you are working multiple events as Shift Lead, separate each day's paperwork and Data Cover Sheet into its own clear pocket folder. Place all folders in the return supply box and return as directed by your manager.
